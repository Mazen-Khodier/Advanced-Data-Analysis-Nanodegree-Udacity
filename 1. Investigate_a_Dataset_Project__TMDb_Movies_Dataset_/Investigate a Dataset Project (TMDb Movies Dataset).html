1. Introduction¶

Films aren't cheap to produce and there are many ideas for new movies everyday. One can not possibly expect to put them all into action and as such, It's often necessary to have a keen insight in such a field to know which ideas are probably wothwhile and which aren't.

In this report, we are going to explore a dataset on movies gathered from the famous website "TMDb". This data includes interesting features like the movies rating, actors, revenue, etc.

We would like to study this dataset closely in an attempt to find out if it's possible to guess whether a movie is probable to be successful or not; or at the very least determine if there are some features that increases the chances of success for an upcoming film. In other words, we want to study the effects of the properties of a movie on its revenue.

Specific questions we would like to know about:¶

    1- What is the average length of a movie?
    2- Do overpriced, high-budget movies get more revenue?
    3- Does the runtime of a movie affect its ratings?
    4- Which companies seem to dominate the industry?
    5- Is the income of a movie heavily affected by its cast?
    6- Which genres are more popular overall?

We plot the popularity of each genre during the period from 1960 till 2015

In [17]:
# defining a function for plotting a certain feature against all genres over the time period of our data with approptiate formatting
def genres_all_years(feature, y_label, graph_title):   
    s = df.copy()
    plt_genre = s['genres'].str.split('|', expand=True).stack().to_frame(name='genre')
    plt_genre.index = plt_genre.index.droplevel(1)
    plt_genre = plt_genre.join(s[feature]).groupby('genre').sum()
    plt = plt_genre.plot(kind='bar', title=graph_title)
    plt.set_xlabel('Genre')
    plt.set_ylabel(y_label)

# plotting the popularity of each genre during the period from 1960 till 2015
genres_all_years('popularity', 'Popularity', 'Popularity by Genres from 1960 to 2015')

We see that the top 5 most popular genres are:

    1- Drama
    2- Action, Thriller, Comedy
    3- Adventure

However, as mentioned above, the actual equation for popularity or any explanation regarding its values are nowhere to be found. As such, we may instead find it more appealing to try to use the revenue feature instead as a reference to the popularity

4. Conclusions¶

In this report, we had a dataset from a movie database and wanted to study the effect of various features on the revenue. Unforetunately, we had to drop a multitude of features from our original dataset which hindered us from answering some of our proposed questions.

We cleaned our data by dropping unnecessary feautres that won't contribute to our analysis, dropped those of intrest but with too much missing data, dropped entries (movies) with missing data and removed duplicates.

After that, using plotting and some summary statistcs we could answer most of the remaining questions:

  • We determined the average timespan of a movie.

  • We saw the relation between movie budget and revenue.

  • We determined that runtime doesn't seem to affect revenue.

  • We determined the top 5 genres of movies from 1960~2015.

Some limitations in the studied dataset were:

    1. Features like cast, keywords, production_companies had too much missing values and were consequently dropped even though we could have used them in our analysis and for answering our questions.

    2. Lack of variety in movies with different ranges of runtime (All were concentrated around 102 minutes).

    3. The popularity column is somewhat vague and no concrete explanation of its values were found; not even online.

    4. Many movies belonged to the same franchise but there were no such classification in the data. For xcample: the data contained a number of "Fast and Furious" movies but they were treated as 2 completely seperate entities, even though the fact of being in the same franchise should be of interest in our analysis (e.g: in studying movies revenue).
